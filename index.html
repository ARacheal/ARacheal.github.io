About Me

Building data solutions
for real-world systems

I'm a Data Analyst at the New York State Metropolitan Transportation Authority (MTA), where I work with real-time transit datasets at scale — processing over 3.5 million records to uncover ridership patterns, diagnose system gaps, and support operational decision-making.

My work spans spatiotemporal analysis of equipment performance, geofence polygon mapping via ArcGIS and Mapbox shared with partners like Uber & Lyft, and studying how weather and seasonal factors impact transit operations.

I'm applying to PhD programs in Data Science, Urban AI, and Responsible Machine Learning for Fall 2026, with a focus on using data-driven methods to improve large-scale urban systems and advance computational urban justice.
